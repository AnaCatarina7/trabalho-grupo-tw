Spice Girls

The Spice Girls are a British pop girl group originally established in 1994. The group is composed of Melanie Brown, Melanie Chisholm, Emma Bunton, Geri Halliwell, and formerly Victoria Beckham. They signed with Virgin Records and rose to fame in 1996 with their debut single, "Wannabe." The single topped the charts in over 30 countries, making the girls a "global phenomenon." Their debut album, Spice, sold 30 million copies, becoming the best-selling album of all time by a female group. The follow-up album, Spiceworld, sold 25 million copies worldwide.
